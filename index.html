Neha Gupta

I am a Social Science and Humanities Research Council Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Geography at Memorial University of Newfoundland, St John's, Newfoundland, Canada. I created this professional website as an update to my web presence at McGill University where I completed my PhD (2012). Prior to McGill, I earned my MSc (2002) in GIS and Spatial Analysis in Archaeology at University College London.

My research focuses on factors that influence long-term change in human societies. To that end, I am interested in the visualization geographically-referenced information in archaeology, particularly digital and computational methods that address the disjuncture between field collection of archaeological data and its integration with digital sources of information. I employ geospatial and geovisualization methods in my current field projects.

Map Indian Archaeology

Capstone for Michigan State University’s Institute for Digital Archaeology. I develop a Web-based dynamic platform that maps Indian archaeology through time and can enable linking with other dynamic and static geographically-referenced sources of information such as newspapers, journal articles, archaeological data and reports.

Insights from the 'Cod Road': Archaeology of St Pierre and Miquelon

This project examines the changing role of Saint Pierre and Miquelon in the French colonial world during the 17th and 18th centuries. The islands, located in the North Atlantic, are French provinces that have been continually settled since the 1600s.

View details »

Northern Odisha Archaeological Project

This is a Shastri-funded project. NOAP employs geospatial and geovisualization methods to gain insight into long-term societal change in the pattern and distribution of settlement in the uplands and coast in the Baitarani River Valley.
